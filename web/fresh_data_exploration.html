üî¨ Scientific Discovery: Data Freshness Matters!

During field verification, we discovered a critical insight: Our initial ALKIS cadastre data was predominantly from 2013 (12 years old). This is actually a common problem in urban planning - official cadastre data often lags by years.

Our solution: We created a "Fresh Data" filter that only trusts locations in areas where data has been updated since 2020. This reduced our dataset from 114,982 to 13,882 locations (12.1%), but dramatically increased reliability for field deployment.

üî¨ Our Fresh Data Methodology

1

Identify Fresh Zones

Analyzed ALKIS 'aktualit' timestamps to find buildings and roads updated since 2020. Found 2,803 fresh buildings (10.4%) and 145 fresh roads (21%).

2

Create Verification Zones

Created 50-meter buffer zones around all fresh data points. These are "verified current" areas where we trust the cadastre data.

3

Filter with Full Exclusions

Started with our full 114,982 locations (which already exclude ALL buildings, roads, trees, etc.) and kept only locations within fresh zones.

4

Re-rank and Enhance

Re-ranked the 13,882 verified locations, added enhanced metadata (species recommendations, social impact, cooling estimates), and exported for field verification.

5

Field Validation Ready

These locations are now ready for real-world verification. They represent areas where our data is current and our predictions are most reliable.
